1.  GSNS course September (information for 2023-2024)

Date and time: 
Round 1: Tuesday 6 September 2023, 13:30 - 15:00.
Round 2: Tuesday 6 September 2023, 15:30 - 17:00.
Location: BBG 209.
Register attendance: Register your attendance.
Exercises: LaTeX_exercises_GSNS_2023-09.pdf
Slides: LaTeX_slides_GSNS_2023-09.pdf
Example of what you achieve in this workshop: LaTeX_example_GSNS_2023-02.pdf
Organization: This course is part of the GSNS introduction Registration for the workshop, and location of the workshop are aranged by them.
Questions: Go to Contact page.
Description:

LaTeX is a code language for typesetting documents, for example your thesis, scientific paper, or hand-in. It produces professional looking documents and typesets mathematical expressions well, making it the standard (and often requirement) for academic documents.

This workshop requires no prior knowledge of LaTeX and will help you through the rough start which new users of LaTeX experience. We show you how to correctly format text and how to include mathematical expressions, tables and images.

For this workshop we will use Overleaf. You can create a free account on Overleaf.com. Please bring a laptop since the workshop will include exercises.

This workshop is organised by the TeXniCie committee of study association A-Eskwadraat. We facilitate use of LaTeX for exact science studies, by organizing extracurricular courses. Information about our activities can be found on our website texnicie.nl.

Question & Answer

Question: References don't appear in the bibliography. Biber shows this reference as (book, label). How do I fix it?
Answer: Set Biber as the default bibliography manager (see thesis manual). With some older LaTeX distributions Biber is missing. Biber is often installed in C:\Program Files\MiKTeX 2.9\miktex\bin\x64. Is it missing? Update MiKTeX, or, in the worst case, reinstall MiKTeX.
